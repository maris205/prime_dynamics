## Abstract (single paragraph, 292 words)

We study the symbolic dynamics of the Eratosthenes sieve under the Metropolis–Stein–Stein (MSS) kneading admissibility framework. The sieve stage Q_k, generated by the symbolic operator S_p = R L^{p-1} acting on the first k primes, naturally matches itineraries of the one-dimensional unimodal map x ↦ 1 − u x² at the band-merging parameter u_c ≈ 1.5436890. We show that this correspondence is obstructed at finite stages: by explicit MSS comparison, the sieve word W_k = Q_k[0, p_{k+1}²) violates admissibility at k = 3 (defect at n = 31) and at k = 5 (defect at the prime gap 113–127), and these failures cannot be repaired by tightening any classical bound on prime gaps. Our central result, the Parity-Gap Lemma, reduces topological admissibility of W_k to an extremal prime-gap inequality G(p_{k+1}²) < p_{k+1} − 1. Classical bounds — Legendre's, Andrica's, the Riemann Hypothesis, and Baker–Harman–Pintz — yield maximal gaps at x = p² exceeding the topological shield p − 1 and are insufficient to guarantee admissibility; only a strongly sub-root bound such as Cramér's conjecture G(x) = O(log² x) forces eventual admissibility for all k above a finite threshold k_0 (numerically k_0 = 6 for k ≤ 5000). Independently, we establish asymptotic admissibility: the topological-defect density ρ(N) vanishes as N → ∞. A four-state parity-split chain then forces an asymptotic geometric decay μ_{2m+2}/μ_{2m} → p_∞ ≈ 0.596 of the even-gap measure, ruling out internal mod-3 resonance and identifying the unimodal projection as an abelian / mod-2 holographic image of the prime distribution. We close with three open problems on arithmetic shadowing for prime constellations, non-autonomous kneading metrics, and sub-quadratic admissibility verifiers.
