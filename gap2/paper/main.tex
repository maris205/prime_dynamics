\begin{abstract}
We prove a sequential (non-autonomous) Birkhoff ergodic theorem for
compositions of unimodal maps $f_{u_n}(x) = 1 - u_n x^2$ whose
parameters drift slowly to a Misiurewicz value $u_c$. Working under an
explicit uniform-inducing-scheme hypothesis---a uniform Young tower
with uniform Lasota--Yorke constants and Keller--Liverani spectral
stability across the relevant parameter set---we show that for every
bounded-variation observable, the time average along the sequential
orbit converges in mean (indeed in $L^2$, hence in probability) to the
spatial average against the unique absolutely continuous invariant
measure $\mu_{u_c}$ of the limiting autonomous map, whenever the drift
tail $\sup_{k\ge n}|u_k - u_c|$ tends to zero. For the logarithmic
schedule $|u_n - u_c| \le c\,(\log n)^{-\beta}$ we further obtain
almost-everywhere convergence when $\beta > 1$, the threshold at which
the second-moment estimate becomes summable along a ratio-one
subsequence. The proof passes to the induced first-return map, where
the dynamics is uniformly expanding, and combines a uniform spectral
gap with a Cesàro estimate of the accumulated drift error. The result
supplies the ergodic foundation---convergence of symbolic cylinder
frequencies to the band-merging statistics---for non-autonomous
symbolic models of arithmetic sequences; we delimit precisely what it
does and does not yield for the associated density envelopes.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\subsection{Sequential dynamical systems and slow drift}

Let $f_u(x) = 1 - u x^2$ be the standard unimodal family on $[-1,1]$.
A \emph{sequential} (or non-autonomous) dynamical system is generated
by composing different members of the family along a parameter
schedule $(u_n)_{n\ge 1}$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cocycle}
  \Phi_N(x) = f_{u_N}\circ f_{u_{N-1}}\circ\cdots\circ f_{u_1}(x).
\end{equation}
When $u_n \equiv u$ is constant the classical Birkhoff theorem governs
the statistics of $\Phi_N$. When $u_n$ varies, even slowly, the orbit
no longer lives on a single attractor and Birkhoff does not apply
directly. This paper establishes a Birkhoff-type law of large numbers
when $u_n$ drifts to a fixed limit $u_c$ at a logarithmic rate.

\subsection{Motivation}

Non-autonomous unimodal schedules of exactly this type arise in recent
symbolic-dynamical models of arithmetic sequences, where a slowly
``aging'' parameter $u_n = u_c - c\,(\log n)^{-2}$ is tuned so that the
symbolic $L$-frequency of the orbit matches a prescribed density
envelope (for instance the prime density $1/\log n$). Such models
posit, but do not prove, that ergodic averages along the drifting orbit
converge to the autonomous limit. The present paper supplies that
missing ergodic foundation in a self-contained form, independent of any
number-theoretic application.

\subsection{Main result}

We work with a one-dimensional family $(f_u)_{u\in U}$ over a parameter
window $U$ and a limit value $u_c$. Our results are conditional on a
structural hypothesis---the existence of a \emph{uniform inducing
scheme}---which isolates exactly the ergodic input the argument needs
and which, at a Misiurewicz limit, is the content of the
Keller--Liverani / Young-tower theory.

\begin{assumption}[Uniform inducing scheme]
\label{ass:uis}
There is a reference domain $A$, a first-return (inducing) construction
$F_u:A\to A$ for each $u\in U$, and a Banach space
$(\mathcal{B}, \|\cdot\|_\mathcal{B})$ compactly embedded in $L^1(m)$,
such that, with constants independent of $u\in U$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(U1)}] each $F_u$ is uniformly expanding with bounded
distortion and a countable Markov partition with uniform exponential
return-time tails (a uniform Young tower);
\item[\textup{(U2)}] the induced transfer operators
$\mathcal{L}_u$ satisfy a uniform Lasota--Yorke inequality on
$\mathcal{B}$, hence a uniform spectral gap: a simple leading
eigenvalue $1$ with density $h_u$ and a uniform contraction
$\|\mathcal{L}_u^n\varphi - h_u\!\int\varphi\,dm\|_\mathcal{B}
\le C\theta^n\|\varphi\|_\mathcal{B}$, $\theta<1$;
\item[\textup{(U3)}] (Keller--Liverani stability) there are $C>0$ and
$\gamma\in(0,1]$ with
$\vertiii{\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c}} \le C|u-u_c|$ in the
Keller--Liverani operator distance, hence
$\|h_u - h_{u_c}\|_{L^1}\le C|u-u_c|^\gamma$;
\item[\textup{(U4)}] the induced spectral gap lifts, through the tower,
to a uniform decay-of-correlations bound \eqref{eq:doc} for the raw
maps $f_u$ on $\mathcal{B}$, and the raw transfer operators $P_u$
inherit the Lipschitz stability
$\|(P_u-P_v)g\|_{L^1}\le C|u-v|\,\|g\|_\mathcal{B}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{assumption}

For the drift schedule we use the monotone-free tail quantity
\[
  \Delta_m \;:=\; \sup_{k\ge m}|u_k - u_c|,
\]
which decreases to $0$ whenever $u_n\to u_c$, with no monotonicity
assumption on $(u_n)$ itself. For the logarithmic schedule
$|u_n-u_c|\le c(\log n)^{-\beta}$ one has $\Delta_m = O((\log m)^{-\beta})$.

\begin{theorem}[Conditional sequential Birkhoff, mean form]
\label{thm:main}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:uis}, suppose $u_n\to u_c$ (equivalently
$\Delta_m\to0$) with all $u_n\in U$. Then for every
$\varphi\in\mathcal{B}$ and every initial law $\nu$ with density in
$\mathcal{B}$,
\[
  \mathbb{E}_\nu\!\left[\frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=1}^{N}
       \varphi(\Phi_n)\right]
  \;\xrightarrow[N\to\infty]{}\;
  \int \varphi\,d\mu_{u_c},
\]
and moreover $\frac1N\sum_{n\le N}\varphi(\Phi_n)\to\int\varphi\,d\mu_{u_c}$
in $L^2(\nu)$ (hence in probability).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Almost-everywhere convergence for $\beta>1$]
\label{cor:ae}
If in addition the schedule satisfies $|u_n-u_c|\le c(\log n)^{-\beta}$
with $\beta>1$, then for every $\varphi\in\mathcal{B}$ the convergence
in Theorem~\ref{thm:main} holds for Lebesgue-almost-every initial
condition.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Misiurewicz instance]
\label{cor:mis}
Let $u_c$ be a Misiurewicz parameter of $f_u(x)=1-ux^2$ and let
$U$ be a one-sided parameter set on which the Young-tower construction
of \cite{young1992decay} is uniform. Then Assumption~\ref{ass:uis}
holds on $U$, and Theorem~\ref{thm:main} and Corollary~\ref{cor:ae}
apply to any schedule $u_n\to u_c$ within $U$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}
We state convergence \emph{in mean / in probability} unconditionally in
the drift rate (Theorem~\ref{thm:main}), and almost-everywhere only for
$\beta>1$ (Corollary~\ref{cor:ae}). The restriction $\beta>1$ is an
honest limitation of the second-moment method used here: the variance
decays only like $(\log N)^{-\beta}$, which is summable along a
ratio-one subsequence precisely when $\beta>1$. Whether a.e.\
convergence persists for $0<\beta\le1$ is left open; it would require a
maximal inequality or martingale approximation beyond the present
scope.
\end{remark}

The proof occupies Sections~\ref{sec:spectral}--\ref{sec:birkhoff}.
Section~\ref{sec:induced} records, for the Misiurewicz instance, why a
\emph{one-sided} parameter set supports the uniform inducing scheme of
Assumption~\ref{ass:uis}; we do not claim uniformity on a full
two-sided neighbourhood of the band-merging point.

\section{The induced first-return map (Misiurewicz instance)}
\label{sec:induced}

This section verifies Assumption~\ref{ass:uis} for the quadratic family
near a Misiurewicz parameter $u_c$, on a suitable parameter set $U$.
We emphasise at the outset that we do \emph{not} claim uniformity on a
full two-sided neighbourhood of $u_c$: the band-merging value is a
bifurcation point, and the attractor topology, periodic windows, and
critical-orbit returns are generally not stable across an open interval
around it. Instead we take $U$ to be a parameter set---in the
applications of interest, a one-sided sequence $u_n\uparrow u_c$ or a
positive-measure Benedicks--Carleson-type set accumulating at
$u_c$---on which a uniform Young tower exists. On such a set the
inducing scheme below has $u$-uniform constants; off such a set
Assumption~\ref{ass:uis} is simply not assumed.

Let $A = \{x < 0\}$ denote the left branch and, for $x \in A$, let
$\tau_u(x) = \min\{ n \geq 1 : f_u^n(x) \in A \}$ be the first-return
time to $A$ under $f_u$, with induced map
$F_u : A \to A$, $F_u(x) = f_u^{\tau_u(x)}(x)$.

\begin{lemma}[Uniform inducing on a tower-supporting set]
\label{lem:induced}
Let $U$ be a parameter set accumulating at $u_c$ on which the family
$(f_u)$ admits a uniform Young tower in the sense of
\cite{young1992decay}: uniform expansion, uniform bounded distortion,
and uniform exponential return-time tails for the first-return map to a
fixed reference interval. Then, with constants independent of $u\in U$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(i)}] $A$ carries a countable Markov partition
$\{A_j^u\}_{j \geq 1}$, $A_j^u = \{x \in A : \tau_u(x) = r_j\}$, on
each piece of which $F_u$ is a $C^2$ diffeomorphism onto $A$;
\item[\textup{(ii)}] $|F_u'| \geq \lambda > 1$ on $\bigcup_j A_j^u$;
\item[\textup{(iii)}] $F_u$ has uniformly bounded distortion;
\item[\textup{(iv)}] the return-time tails are uniformly exponential:
$m(\tau_u > r) \le C\,\kappa^{r}$ for some $\kappa<1$, $C$, uniform in
$u\in U$.
\end{enumerate}
In particular (U1) of Assumption~\ref{ass:uis} holds on $U$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
At $u_c$ the critical orbit is pre-periodic and bounded away from
$\{x=0\}$ after time one, so by Ma\~n\'e's theorem
\cite[Thm.\ A]{mane1985hyperbolicity} there are $C,\lambda_0>1$ with
$|(f_{u_c}^n)'(x)|\ge C\lambda_0^n$ for orbits avoiding a fixed
neighbourhood of $0$, giving (i)--(iii) at $u_c$ exactly as in the
autonomous theory \cite[Ch.\ III]{demelo1993one}, and an acim $\mu_{u_c}$
with $\mathrm{BV}$ density \cite[Thm.\ 1]{misiurewicz1981absolutely}.
The point of the hypothesis on $U$ is precisely that these estimates
persist with $u$-uniform constants: this is the content of the
Benedicks--Carleson / Young tower construction, which produces a
positive-measure parameter set accumulating at $u_c$ on which the tower
data---expansion rate, distortion constant, and tail exponent---are
uniform \cite{young1992decay}. We invoke that construction rather than
re-prove it, and on the resulting $U$ properties (i)--(iv) hold by
definition of the uniform tower. We do \emph{not} assert that $U$
contains an interval around $u_c$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
\label{rem:even-returns}
At $u_c$ the kneading sequence $RLR^\infty$ forces every return time
$\tau_{u_c}$ to be even, so $r_j = 2j$. For $u$ near $u_c$ the partition
is a finite recoding of this one; the parity structure is not needed
below, only the uniform expansion and distortion of
Lemma~\ref{lem:induced}.
\end{remark}

\section{Spectral stability under the drift}
\label{sec:spectral}

Let $\mathcal{L}_u$ denote the Perron--Frobenius (transfer) operator of
the induced map $F_u$ acting on $\mathrm{BV}(A)$,
\[
  \mathcal{L}_u \varphi(y)
  = \sum_{x \in F_u^{-1}(y)} \frac{\varphi(x)}{|F_u'(x)|}.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Uniform spectral gap]
\label{lem:gap}
There are constants $C_1 > 0$, $0 < \theta < 1$, independent of
$u \in U$, such that each $\mathcal{L}_u$ has a simple leading
eigenvalue $1$ with strictly positive $\mathrm{BV}$ eigenfunction
$h_u$ (the density of the induced acim $\mu_u^A$), and
\[
  \bigl\| \mathcal{L}_u^n \varphi - h_u \textstyle\int \varphi \,dm \bigr\|_{\mathrm{BV}}
  \leq C_1 \theta^n \|\varphi\|_{\mathrm{BV}}
  \qquad (n \geq 0,\ u \in U).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:induced} each $F_u$ is a piecewise-$C^2$, uniformly
expanding ($|F_u'| \geq \lambda > 1$) Markov map with uniformly bounded
distortion, the constants being independent of $u \in U$. The
Lasota--Yorke inequality \cite{lasota1973existence} then holds with
$u$-uniform constants: there are $\rho \in (0,1)$ and $D > 0$ with
\[
  \operatorname{Var}(\mathcal{L}_u \varphi)
  \leq \rho \operatorname{Var}(\varphi) + D \|\varphi\|_{L^1}
  \qquad (u \in U).
\]
By the Ionescu-Tulcea--Marinescu / Hennion argument
\cite[Ch.\ III]{baladi2000positive}, each $\mathcal{L}_u$ is
quasi-compact on $\mathrm{BV}(A)$ with essential spectral radius
$\leq \rho$; topological mixing of $F_u$ gives a simple leading
eigenvalue $1$ and no other peripheral spectrum, hence a spectral gap.
The uniformity of $\rho, D$ across $U$ makes the gap and the constants
$C_1, \theta$ uniform.
\end{proof}

We now quantify how $\mathcal{L}_u$ moves as $u$ varies. Following
Keller--Liverani \cite{kellerliverani1999}, equip the pair
$(\mathrm{BV}(A), L^1(m))$ with the triple norm and measure operator
distance by
\[
  \vertiii{\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c}}
  = \sup\{ \|(\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c})\varphi\|_{L^1}
           : \|\varphi\|_{\mathrm{BV}} \leq 1 \}.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Keller--Liverani stability, induced and raw]
\label{lem:kl}
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:uis} there are constants $C_2 > 0$ and
$\gamma \in (0,1]$, independent of $u\in U$, such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(a)}] (induced) $\vertiii{\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c}}
\leq C_2 |u - u_c|$, hence $\|h_u - h_{u_c}\|_{L^1} \leq C_2 |u - u_c|^{\gamma}$;
\item[\textup{(b)}] (raw) the raw transfer operators $P_u$ of $f_u$
satisfy $\|(P_u - P_{u_c})g\|_{L^1} \leq C_2 |u - u_c|\,\|g\|_{\mathcal{B}}$
for all $g\in\mathcal{B}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(a)} The branch derivatives $|F_u'|$ and the partition endpoints
depend Lipschitz-continuously on $u$ on each fixed cylinder
\cite[Ch.\ V]{demelo1993one}; comparing $\mathcal{L}_u$ and
$\mathcal{L}_{u_c}$ on a fixed $\varphi$ in $L^1$ gives the
operator-distance bound. The Keller--Liverani theorem
\cite[Thm.\ 1]{kellerliverani1999}, whose hypotheses are exactly the
uniform Lasota--Yorke bound of Lemma~\ref{lem:gap} (U2) together with
this operator-distance estimate (U3), yields Hölder continuity of the
leading eigenprojection, hence
$\|h_u - h_{u_c}\|_{L^1} \leq C_2 |u-u_c|^{\gamma}$ for some
$\gamma\in(0,1]$ determined by the gap relative to the Lasota--Yorke
constants. We do not compute $\gamma$ analytically; the numerical fit
of Section~\ref{sec:numerics} is consistent with $\gamma\approx\tfrac12$,
as one would expect from the quadratic tangency at the critical point,
but we use only $\gamma>0$ below.

\emph{(b)} This is the raw-operator clause (U4) of
Assumption~\ref{ass:uis}, which we now justify rather than merely posit.
Write the raw evolution through the tower: for $g\in\mathcal{B}$,
$P_u^{\,r}g$ decomposes over the return-time partition as a sum
$\sum_j \mathbf{1}_{f_u^j(A_j^u)}\cdot(\text{branch transfer of }\mathcal{L}_u)$,
and the difference $P_u - P_{u_c}$ acts only through the branch
derivatives and partition endpoints, which are Lipschitz in $u$ on each
cylinder, exactly as in (a). Summability of the contributions is
guaranteed by the uniform exponential tails (U1)(iv). Hence
$\|(P_u-P_{u_c})g\|_{L^1}\le C_2|u-u_c|\,\|g\|_{\mathcal{B}}$, the
$\mathcal{B}$-norm of $g$ entering because the per-cylinder Lipschitz
constants are weighted by $\operatorname{Var}$-type seminorms.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Clause (b) is the step the first draft of this work glossed over: the
mean estimate of Section~\ref{sec:birkhoff} compares the \emph{raw}
operators $P_{u_k}$, whereas the spectral gap is naturally stated for
the \emph{induced} operators $\mathcal{L}_u$. The two are linked only
through the tower, and the uniform exponential tails (U1)(iv) are what
make the raw Lipschitz bound (b) follow from the induced bound (a). We
have therefore promoted (b) to an explicit clause of
Assumption~\ref{ass:uis} and indicated its tower derivation, rather
than asserting it silently.
\end{remark}

\section{Drift accumulation and almost-everywhere convergence}
\label{sec:birkhoff}

We now prove Theorem~\ref{thm:main}. Throughout, $\varphi \in \mathrm{BV}[-1,1]$
is fixed with $\int \varphi \, d\mu_{u_c} = 0$ (subtract the mean; the
general case follows by linearity), and $\bar\varphi_N(x)
= \frac1N\sum_{n=1}^N \varphi(\Phi_n(x))$.

\subsection{Mean estimate}

Let $\nu$ be any probability measure on $[-1,1]$ absolutely continuous
with $\mathrm{BV}$ density, and write $P_u$ for the Perron--Frobenius
operator of the \emph{raw} map $f_u$ on $\mathrm{BV}[-1,1]$. The induced
spectral gap of Lemma~\ref{lem:gap} lifts, through the standard tower
correspondence \cite{young1992decay}, to a decay-of-correlations bound
for $f_u$: there are $\tilde C, \tilde\theta$, uniform in $u \in U$,
with
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:doc}
  \Bigl| \int \varphi \cdot (f_u^n)^* \psi \, dm
        - \int \varphi \, d\mu_u \int \psi \, dm \Bigr|
  \leq \tilde C \tilde\theta^{n} \|\varphi\|_{\mathrm{BV}} \|\psi\|_{L^1}.
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Mean convergence]
\label{prop:mean}
There is $C_3 > 0$ such that for the sequential orbit started from the
reference density $\nu$,
\[
  \Bigl| \mathbb{E}_\nu\bigl[\varphi(\Phi_n)\bigr] \Bigr|
  \;\leq\; C_3 \tilde\theta^{\,n}
         + C_3 \sum_{k=1}^{n} \tilde\theta^{\,n-k}\,|u_k - u_c|.
\]
Consequently $\mathbb{E}_\nu[\varphi(\Phi_n)] \to 0$, and
$\mathbb{E}_\nu[\bar\varphi_N] \to 0$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Write $\Phi_n = f_{u_n} \circ \Phi_{n-1}$ and let $\nu_n = (\Phi_n)_*\nu$
have density $g_n$. Then $g_n = P_{u_n} g_{n-1}$. Compare with the
autonomous transfer at $u_c$: telescoping,
\[
  g_n - h_{u_c}\!\!\int g_n
  = \bigl(P_{u_c}^{\,n} - \text{proj}\bigr) g_0
    + \sum_{k=1}^{n} P_{u_c}^{\,n-k}\,(P_{u_k} - P_{u_c})\, g_{k-1}.
\]
The first term has $L^1$ norm $\leq C \tilde\theta^n$ by
\eqref{eq:doc}. For the $k$-th summand, Lemma~\ref{lem:kl} gives
$\|(P_{u_k} - P_{u_c})g_{k-1}\|_{L^1} \leq C_2 |u_k - u_c|$ (the
densities $g_{k-1}$ have uniformly bounded variation, again by the
uniform Lasota--Yorke bound), and $P_{u_c}^{n-k}$ contracts the
zero-mean part by $\tilde\theta^{n-k}$. Pairing with $\varphi$ and
using $\int \varphi\, d\mu_{u_c} = 0$ gives the stated bound.

For the tail: split $\sum_{k\le n}\tilde\theta^{n-k}|u_k-u_c|$ at
$k = \lfloor n/2 \rfloor$. In the low-$k$ block, $\tilde\theta^{n-k}
\le \tilde\theta^{n/2}$ and $|u_k - u_c|$ is decreasing, so
\[
  \sum_{k\le n/2}\tilde\theta^{n-k}|u_k-u_c|
  \;\le\; |u_1 - u_c|\,\tilde\theta^{n/2}\sum_{k\le n/2}\tilde\theta^{\,n/2-k}
  \;\le\; \frac{|u_1 - u_c|}{1-\tilde\theta}\,\tilde\theta^{\,n/2}
  \;\to\; 0.
\]
The high-$k$ block is
$\sum_{n/2 < k \le n}\tilde\theta^{n-k}|u_k-u_c|
\le \bigl(\sup_{k>n/2}|u_k-u_c|\bigr)\sum_{j\ge0}\tilde\theta^{\,j}
\le c\,(\log(n/2))^{-\beta}/(1-\tilde\theta) \to 0$. Hence each term,
and the Cesàro mean, vanish.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Almost-everywhere upgrade}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
By Proposition~\ref{prop:mean} the means $\mathbb{E}_\nu[\bar\varphi_N]
\to 0$. To pass to almost-everywhere convergence we bound the variance.
The same telescoping applied to the two-point function
$\mathbb{E}_\nu[\varphi(\Phi_m)\varphi(\Phi_n)]$, $m < n$, together with
\eqref{eq:doc}, yields
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cov}
  \bigl|\operatorname{Cov}_\nu(\varphi\circ\Phi_m,\,\varphi\circ\Phi_n)\bigr|
  \leq C_4\,\tilde\theta^{\,n-m} + \varepsilon_m,
\end{equation}
where the geometric term comes from the decay of correlations over the
$n-m$ steps after time $m$, and
$\varepsilon_m = C_4 \sup_{k\ge m}|u_k - u_c| = O\!\bigl((\log m)^{-\beta}\bigr)$
collects the drift contributions accumulated up to time $m$ (which
control how far the law of $\Phi_m$ sits from $\mu_{u_c}$).

Summing \eqref{eq:cov} over the $N^2$ pairs,
\[
  \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_N)
  = \frac{1}{N^2}\sum_{m,n=1}^{N}\operatorname{Cov}_\nu(\varphi\circ\Phi_m,\varphi\circ\Phi_n)
  \le \frac{2}{N^2}\sum_{m=1}^{N}\sum_{j\ge 0} C_4\tilde\theta^{\,j}
      \;+\; \frac{2}{N^2}\sum_{m=1}^{N} N\,\varepsilon_m.
\]
The first sum is $\le \frac{2C_4}{N(1-\tilde\theta)} = O(1/N)$. The
second is $\frac{2}{N}\sum_{m\le N}\varepsilon_m$; since
$\varepsilon_m = C_4\Delta_m \to 0$, its Cesàro mean
$\frac1N\sum_{m\le N}\varepsilon_m \to 0$, with rate
$O((\log N)^{-\beta})$ in the logarithmic case. Hence
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:var}
  \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_N) = O\!\Bigl(\tfrac1N\textstyle\sum_{m\le N}\Delta_m\Bigr) \to 0,
\end{equation}
which is $O((\log N)^{-\beta})$ under the logarithmic schedule. Together
with $\mathbb{E}_\nu[\bar\varphi_N]\to0$ from Proposition~\ref{prop:mean},
this gives $\bar\varphi_N\to\int\varphi\,d\mu_{u_c}$ in $L^2(\nu)$, hence
in probability. This proves Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:ae}]
We upgrade the $L^2$ convergence to almost-everywhere convergence when
$\beta>1$. By \eqref{eq:var} the logarithmic schedule gives
$\operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_N) \le C_5(\log N)^{-\beta}$. Choose
an exponent $a$ with $1/\beta < a < 1$, which is possible exactly
because $\beta>1$, and set $N_i = \lfloor \exp(i^{\,a})\rfloor$. Then
\[
  \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_{N_i})
  \le C_5 (\log N_i)^{-\beta} = C_5\, i^{-a\beta},
  \qquad a\beta>1,
\]
so $\sum_i \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_{N_i})<\infty$. Since
$\mathbb{E}_\nu[\bar\varphi_{N_i}]\to0$, Chebyshev and Borel--Cantelli
give $\bar\varphi_{N_i}\to\int\varphi\,d\mu_{u_c}$ $\nu$-a.e.

For the gaps, let $N_i\le N<N_{i+1}$. As $\varphi\in\mathcal{B}$ is
bounded,
\[
  \bigl|\bar\varphi_N - \tfrac{N_i}{N}\bar\varphi_{N_i}\bigr|
  \le \frac{N_{i+1}-N_i}{N_i}\,\|\varphi\|_\infty.
\]
Because $a<1$,
$\log N_{i+1}-\log N_i = (i+1)^a - i^a = O(i^{a-1}) = o(1)$, so
$N_{i+1}/N_i\to1$ and likewise $N_i/N\to1$. Hence the gap term vanishes
and $\bar\varphi_N\to\int\varphi\,d\mu_{u_c}$ $\nu$-a.e. The two
conditions $a\beta>1$ and $a<1$ are simultaneously satisfiable
\emph{iff} $\beta>1$, which is the source of the restriction; for
$0<\beta\le1$ the second-moment method gives only the $L^2$ conclusion
of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}.

Since $\nu$ was an arbitrary probability measure with density in
$\mathcal{B}$, and these are mutually equivalent to Lebesgue on the
attractor, the convergence holds Lebesgue-a.e.
\end{proof}

\section{Numerical verification}
\label{sec:numerics}

We verify the two analytic ingredients directly; full scripts are in
the accompanying repository.

\paragraph{Density stability (Lemma~\ref{lem:kl}).}
Estimating $h_u$ by a $10^7$-step orbit histogram for
$u = u_c - \Delta$, $\Delta \in \{10^{-3},\dots,5\times10^{-2}\}$,
and comparing in $L^1$ to $h_{u_c}$, a log--log fit gives
\[
  \|h_u - h_{u_c}\|_{L^1} \sim |u - u_c|^{0.51},
\]
consistent with a Hölder modulus $\gamma\approx\tfrac12$ in
Lemma~\ref{lem:kl} (Figure~\ref{fig:gap2}(a)). We stress that this is a
sanity check on the density-stability input, not a verification of the
exponent, which our proof leaves as some $\gamma\in(0,1]$.

\paragraph{Sequential Birkhoff convergence (Theorem~\ref{thm:main}).}
For $\varphi = \mathbf{1}_{\{x<0\}}$ and the schedule
$u_n = u_c - c(\log n)^{-\beta}$, the time average
$\bar\varphi_N$ approaches the autonomous value
$\int\varphi\,d\mu_{u_c} \approx 0.2207$. The error decreases with $N$
and the rate increases with $\beta$: at $c=1$, $\beta=1$, $N=2\times10^7$
the error is $3.8\times10^{-3}$ (Figure~\ref{fig:gap2}(b)), consistent
with the $\tfrac1N\sum_{m\le N}\Delta_m$ envelope of
Proposition~\ref{prop:mean}. These are sanity checks on the
\emph{mean} convergence of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}; they do not, and
cannot, verify the almost-everywhere statement of
Corollary~\ref{cor:ae}, which concerns a Lebesgue-full set of initial
conditions.

\section{Concluding remarks}
\label{sec:remarks}

The schedule rate enters only through the tail
$\Delta_m = \sup_{k\ge m}|u_k - u_c|$, so the \emph{mean} convergence of
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} holds for every drift with $\Delta_m\to0$,
including all logarithmic rates $\beta>0$ and all polynomial rates; the
almost-everywhere statement (Corollary~\ref{cor:ae}) is established here
only for $\beta>1$, the threshold at which the variance
$O(\tfrac1N\sum_{m\le N}\Delta_m)$ becomes summable along a ratio-one
subsequence. Closing the gap $0<\beta\le1$ for a.e.\ convergence would
require a maximal inequality or a martingale approximation, which we do
not pursue.

\paragraph{Scope of the application.}
The logarithmic case is highlighted because it is the one arising in
non-autonomous symbolic models of arithmetic densities
\cite{wang2026emergence}, where an aging parameter
$u_n = u_c - c(\log n)^{-2}$ is tuned to a $1/\log n$ density envelope.
It is important to delimit precisely what the present theorem supplies
there. Theorem~\ref{thm:main} shows that the \emph{Cesàro averages} of
bounded observables along the drifting orbit converge to the autonomous
$u_c$-average; equivalently, the symbolic cylinder frequencies of the
drifting orbit asymptotically inherit the band-merging statistics
$\mu_{u_c}$. This is the ergodic foundation that the symbolic model had
assumed rather than proved.

It does \emph{not}, by itself, produce the $1/\log n$ \emph{density
envelope} of the raw arithmetic sequence: an ordinary Birkhoff average
converges to a constant, whereas the prime-density envelope is a
non-stationary, time-decaying quantity. Recovering the envelope requires
an additional non-stationary ingredient---a shrinking-target or
weighted/thinned estimator, or an externally imposed aging mechanism---
which lies outside the scope of this paper. We make this boundary
explicit to avoid conflating the two statements: what is proved here is
the convergence of stationary cylinder frequencies, not the emergence of
a non-stationary density profile.

We expect the method (inducing $+$ uniform Lasota--Yorke $+$
Keller--Liverani $+$ second-moment subsequence argument) to extend to
multimodal and higher-dimensional piecewise-expanding families admitting
a uniform inducing scheme in the sense of Assumption~\ref{ass:uis}
across the relevant parameter set.
