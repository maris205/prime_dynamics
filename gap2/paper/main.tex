\begin{abstract}
We prove a sequential (non-autonomous) Birkhoff ergodic theorem for
compositions of unimodal maps $f_{u_n}(x) = 1 - u_n x^2$ whose
parameters drift slowly to a Misiurewicz value $u_c$. Under the
logarithmic rate $|u_n - u_c| \le c\,(\log n)^{-\beta}$ with
$\beta > 0$, we show that for Lebesgue-almost-every initial condition
and every bounded-variation observable, the time average along the
sequential orbit converges to the spatial average against the unique
absolutely continuous invariant measure $\mu_{u_c}$ of the limiting
autonomous map. The proof works on the induced first-return map to the
left branch, where the map is uniformly expanding, and combines the
Keller--Liverani spectral-stability theorem with a Cesàro estimate of
the accumulated drift error. The result supplies the ergodic
foundation for non-autonomous symbolic models of arithmetic sequences,
in which a slowly aging parameter is used to match a target density
envelope.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\subsection{Sequential dynamical systems and slow drift}

Let $f_u(x) = 1 - u x^2$ be the standard unimodal family on $[-1,1]$.
A \emph{sequential} (or non-autonomous) dynamical system is generated
by composing different members of the family along a parameter
schedule $(u_n)_{n\ge 1}$,
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cocycle}
  \Phi_N(x) = f_{u_N}\circ f_{u_{N-1}}\circ\cdots\circ f_{u_1}(x).
\end{equation}
When $u_n \equiv u$ is constant the classical Birkhoff theorem governs
the statistics of $\Phi_N$. When $u_n$ varies, even slowly, the orbit
no longer lives on a single attractor and Birkhoff does not apply
directly. This paper establishes a Birkhoff-type law of large numbers
when $u_n$ drifts to a fixed limit $u_c$ at a logarithmic rate.

\subsection{Motivation}

Non-autonomous unimodal schedules of exactly this type arise in recent
symbolic-dynamical models of arithmetic sequences, where a slowly
``aging'' parameter $u_n = u_c - c\,(\log n)^{-2}$ is tuned so that the
symbolic $L$-frequency of the orbit matches a prescribed density
envelope (for instance the prime density $1/\log n$). Such models
posit, but do not prove, that ergodic averages along the drifting orbit
converge to the autonomous limit. The present paper supplies that
missing ergodic foundation in a self-contained form, independent of any
number-theoretic application.

\subsection{Main result}

We work at a Misiurewicz parameter $u_c$: the critical orbit of
$f_{u_c}$ is pre-periodic and lands on a repelling cycle, so $f_{u_c}$
admits a unique absolutely continuous invariant probability measure
$\mu_{u_c}$ with bounded-variation density, and is non-uniformly
expanding with the only critical point at $x=0$.

\begin{assumption}[Slow logarithmic drift]
\label{ass:drift}
The schedule $(u_n)$ satisfies $u_n \to u_c$ and, for some $c>0$,
$\beta>0$ and all large $n$,
\[
  |u_n - u_c| \;\le\; \frac{c}{(\log n)^{\beta}}.
\]
We further assume each $u_n$ lies in a fixed neighbourhood
$U$ of $u_c$ throughout which the induced-map spectral gap of
Section~\ref{sec:spectral} is uniform.
\end{assumption}

\begin{theorem}[Sequential Birkhoff theorem]
\label{thm:main}
Let $u_c$ be a Misiurewicz parameter and let $(u_n)$ satisfy
Assumption~\ref{ass:drift}. Then for Lebesgue-almost-every $x_0\in[-1,1]$
and every $\varphi \in \mathrm{BV}[-1,1]$,
\[
  \frac{1}{N}\sum_{n=1}^{N}\varphi\bigl(\Phi_n(x_0)\bigr)
  \;\xrightarrow[N\to\infty]{}\;
  \int_{-1}^{1}\varphi \, d\mu_{u_c}.
\]
\end{theorem}

The proof occupies Sections~\ref{sec:spectral}--\ref{sec:birkhoff} and
has three ingredients, summarised here.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Inducing (Section~\ref{sec:induced}).} The raw maps
$f_{u_n}$ are not uniformly expanding because of the critical point at
$x=0$. We pass to the first-return map $F_{u}$ to the left branch
$A = \{x<0\}$, which is piecewise $C^2$ and uniformly expanding with a
countable Markov partition, uniformly in $u\in U$.

\item \textbf{Spectral stability (Section~\ref{sec:spectral}).} The
Perron--Frobenius operators $\mathcal{L}_{F_u}$ on
$\mathrm{BV}(A)$ have a uniform spectral gap, and by the
Keller--Liverani perturbation theorem the leading data
(eigenvalue $1$, invariant density $h_u$, spectral projection) vary
continuously, indeed Hölder-continuously, in $u$ near $u_c$.

\item \textbf{Drift accumulation and Birkhoff
(Section~\ref{sec:birkhoff}).} Telescoping the sequential orbit against
the autonomous orbit at $u_c$, the accumulated error is governed by
$\sum_{n}|u_n-u_c|$ weighted by the exponential decay of correlations
from the spectral gap. A Cesàro / Gál--Koksma argument then upgrades
the resulting $L^2$ control to almost-everywhere convergence.
\end{enumerate}

\section{The induced first-return map}
\label{sec:induced}

Fix a neighbourhood $U = [u_c - \delta_0, u_c + \delta_0]$ of the
Misiurewicz parameter, with $\delta_0 > 0$ small enough that every
$f_u$, $u \in U$, has a topological attractor on which the critical
orbit stays bounded away from escape. Let $A = \{x < 0\}$ denote the
left branch and, for $x \in A$, let
\[
  \tau_u(x) = \min\{ n \geq 1 : f_u^n(x) \in A \}
\]
be the first-return time of $x$ to $A$ under $f_u$. The induced map is
\[
  F_u : A \to A, \qquad F_u(x) = f_u^{\tau_u(x)}(x).
\]

\begin{lemma}[Uniformly expanding induced map]
\label{lem:induced}
There exist $\lambda > 1$, $K \geq 1$ and a constant $C_0 > 0$,
all independent of $u \in U$, such that for every $u \in U$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[\textup{(i)}] $A$ carries a countable Markov partition
$\{A_j^u\}_{j \geq 1}$, $A_j^u = \{x \in A : \tau_u(x) = r_j\}$, on
each piece of which $F_u$ is a $C^2$ diffeomorphism onto $A$;
\item[\textup{(ii)}] $|F_u'| \geq \lambda > 1$ on $\bigcup_j A_j^u$;
\item[\textup{(iii)}] $F_u$ has uniformly bounded distortion:
$\bigl|\log |F_u'(x)| - \log|F_u'(y)|\bigr| \leq C_0\,|F_u(x) - F_u(y)|$
for $x, y$ in the same partition element.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
At the Misiurewicz parameter $u_c$ the critical orbit is pre-periodic
and bounded away from $\{x = 0\}$ after time one, so by Ma\~n\'e's
theorem \cite[Thm.\ A]{mane1985hyperbolicity} there are $C, \lambda_0 > 1$
with $|(f_{u_c}^n)'(x)| \geq C\lambda_0^n$ for every $x$ whose orbit up
to time $n$ avoids a fixed neighbourhood of $0$. Each return branch
$A_j^{u_c}$ consists of points whose itinerary visits $A$ only at the
endpoints, hence avoids that neighbourhood for the intervening
$r_j - 1$ steps; thus $|F_{u_c}'| \geq C\lambda_0^{r_j} \geq \lambda > 1$
for a uniform $\lambda$. Bounded distortion (iii) follows from the
$C^2$ bound on $f_{u_c}$ along non-critical orbit segments
\cite[Ch.\ III]{demelo1993one}.

These estimates are stable: the constants $C, \lambda_0$ and the
distortion bound depend only on a finite jet of the critical orbit and
on the uniform separation of that orbit from $0$, both of which vary
continuously in $u$. Shrinking $\delta_0$ if necessary, all three
properties hold with $u$-independent constants throughout $U$
\cite[Ch.\ V]{demelo1993one}. The acim $\mu_u$ of $f_u$ exists and has
$\mathrm{BV}$ density by \cite[Thm.\ 1]{misiurewicz1981absolutely}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
\label{rem:even-returns}
At $u_c$ the kneading sequence $RLR^\infty$ forces every return time
$\tau_{u_c}$ to be even, so $r_j = 2j$. For $u$ near $u_c$ the partition
is a finite recoding of this one; the parity structure is not needed
below, only the uniform expansion and distortion of
Lemma~\ref{lem:induced}.
\end{remark}

\section{Spectral stability under the drift}
\label{sec:spectral}

Let $\mathcal{L}_u$ denote the Perron--Frobenius (transfer) operator of
the induced map $F_u$ acting on $\mathrm{BV}(A)$,
\[
  \mathcal{L}_u \varphi(y)
  = \sum_{x \in F_u^{-1}(y)} \frac{\varphi(x)}{|F_u'(x)|}.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Uniform spectral gap]
\label{lem:gap}
There are constants $C_1 > 0$, $0 < \theta < 1$, independent of
$u \in U$, such that each $\mathcal{L}_u$ has a simple leading
eigenvalue $1$ with strictly positive $\mathrm{BV}$ eigenfunction
$h_u$ (the density of the induced acim $\mu_u^A$), and
\[
  \bigl\| \mathcal{L}_u^n \varphi - h_u \textstyle\int \varphi \,dm \bigr\|_{\mathrm{BV}}
  \leq C_1 \theta^n \|\varphi\|_{\mathrm{BV}}
  \qquad (n \geq 0,\ u \in U).
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:induced} each $F_u$ is a piecewise-$C^2$, uniformly
expanding ($|F_u'| \geq \lambda > 1$) Markov map with uniformly bounded
distortion, the constants being independent of $u \in U$. The
Lasota--Yorke inequality \cite{lasota1973existence} then holds with
$u$-uniform constants: there are $\rho \in (0,1)$ and $D > 0$ with
\[
  \operatorname{Var}(\mathcal{L}_u \varphi)
  \leq \rho \operatorname{Var}(\varphi) + D \|\varphi\|_{L^1}
  \qquad (u \in U).
\]
By the Ionescu-Tulcea--Marinescu / Hennion argument
\cite[Ch.\ III]{baladi2000positive}, each $\mathcal{L}_u$ is
quasi-compact on $\mathrm{BV}(A)$ with essential spectral radius
$\leq \rho$; topological mixing of $F_u$ gives a simple leading
eigenvalue $1$ and no other peripheral spectrum, hence a spectral gap.
The uniformity of $\rho, D$ across $U$ makes the gap and the constants
$C_1, \theta$ uniform.
\end{proof}

We now quantify how $\mathcal{L}_u$ moves as $u$ varies. Following
Keller--Liverani \cite{kellerliverani1999}, equip the pair
$(\mathrm{BV}(A), L^1(m))$ with the triple norm and measure operator
distance by
\[
  \vertiii{\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c}}
  = \sup\{ \|(\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c})\varphi\|_{L^1}
           : \|\varphi\|_{\mathrm{BV}} \leq 1 \}.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Keller--Liverani stability with Hölder modulus]
\label{lem:kl}
There are constants $C_2 > 0$ and $\gamma \in (0,1]$, independent of
$u$, such that for all $u \in U$
\[
  \vertiii{\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c}} \leq C_2 |u - u_c|,
  \qquad
  \| h_u - h_{u_c} \|_{L^1} \leq C_2 |u - u_c|^{\gamma}.
\]
Numerically (Section~\ref{sec:numerics}) the density modulus is
$\gamma = \tfrac12$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The branch derivatives $|F_u'|$ and the partition endpoints depend
Lipschitz-continuously on $u$ on each fixed cylinder
\cite[Ch.\ V]{demelo1993one}, so applying $\mathcal{L}_u$ and
$\mathcal{L}_{u_c}$ to a fixed $\varphi \in \mathrm{BV}$ and comparing
in $L^1$ gives the first bound $\vertiii{\mathcal{L}_u - \mathcal{L}_{u_c}}
\leq C_2 |u - u_c|$. The Keller--Liverani perturbation theorem
\cite[Thm.\ 1]{kellerliverani1999}, whose hypotheses are exactly the
uniform Lasota--Yorke bound of Lemma~\ref{lem:gap} together with this
operator-distance estimate, yields Hölder continuity of the leading
eigenprojection, hence of the invariant density:
$\|h_u - h_{u_c}\|_{L^1} \leq C_2 |u - u_c|^{\gamma}$ with
$\gamma$ controlled by the size of the spectral gap relative to the
Lasota--Yorke constants. The quadratic tangency at the critical point
makes the sharp exponent $\gamma = \tfrac12$, in agreement with the
numerical fit of Section~\ref{sec:numerics}.
\end{proof}

\section{Drift accumulation and almost-everywhere convergence}
\label{sec:birkhoff}

We now prove Theorem~\ref{thm:main}. Throughout, $\varphi \in \mathrm{BV}[-1,1]$
is fixed with $\int \varphi \, d\mu_{u_c} = 0$ (subtract the mean; the
general case follows by linearity), and $\bar\varphi_N(x)
= \frac1N\sum_{n=1}^N \varphi(\Phi_n(x))$.

\subsection{Mean estimate}

Let $\nu$ be any probability measure on $[-1,1]$ absolutely continuous
with $\mathrm{BV}$ density, and write $P_u$ for the Perron--Frobenius
operator of the \emph{raw} map $f_u$ on $\mathrm{BV}[-1,1]$. The induced
spectral gap of Lemma~\ref{lem:gap} lifts, through the standard tower
correspondence \cite{young1992decay}, to a decay-of-correlations bound
for $f_u$: there are $\tilde C, \tilde\theta$, uniform in $u \in U$,
with
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:doc}
  \Bigl| \int \varphi \cdot (f_u^n)^* \psi \, dm
        - \int \varphi \, d\mu_u \int \psi \, dm \Bigr|
  \leq \tilde C \tilde\theta^{n} \|\varphi\|_{\mathrm{BV}} \|\psi\|_{L^1}.
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Mean convergence]
\label{prop:mean}
There is $C_3 > 0$ such that for the sequential orbit started from the
reference density $\nu$,
\[
  \Bigl| \mathbb{E}_\nu\bigl[\varphi(\Phi_n)\bigr] \Bigr|
  \;\leq\; C_3 \tilde\theta^{\,n}
         + C_3 \sum_{k=1}^{n} \tilde\theta^{\,n-k}\,|u_k - u_c|.
\]
Consequently $\mathbb{E}_\nu[\varphi(\Phi_n)] \to 0$, and
$\mathbb{E}_\nu[\bar\varphi_N] \to 0$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Write $\Phi_n = f_{u_n} \circ \Phi_{n-1}$ and let $\nu_n = (\Phi_n)_*\nu$
have density $g_n$. Then $g_n = P_{u_n} g_{n-1}$. Compare with the
autonomous transfer at $u_c$: telescoping,
\[
  g_n - h_{u_c}\!\!\int g_n
  = \bigl(P_{u_c}^{\,n} - \text{proj}\bigr) g_0
    + \sum_{k=1}^{n} P_{u_c}^{\,n-k}\,(P_{u_k} - P_{u_c})\, g_{k-1}.
\]
The first term has $L^1$ norm $\leq C \tilde\theta^n$ by
\eqref{eq:doc}. For the $k$-th summand, Lemma~\ref{lem:kl} gives
$\|(P_{u_k} - P_{u_c})g_{k-1}\|_{L^1} \leq C_2 |u_k - u_c|$ (the
densities $g_{k-1}$ have uniformly bounded variation, again by the
uniform Lasota--Yorke bound), and $P_{u_c}^{n-k}$ contracts the
zero-mean part by $\tilde\theta^{n-k}$. Pairing with $\varphi$ and
using $\int \varphi\, d\mu_{u_c} = 0$ gives the stated bound.

For the tail: split $\sum_{k\le n}\tilde\theta^{n-k}|u_k-u_c|$ at
$k = \lfloor n/2 \rfloor$. In the low-$k$ block, $\tilde\theta^{n-k}
\le \tilde\theta^{n/2}$ and $|u_k - u_c|$ is decreasing, so
\[
  \sum_{k\le n/2}\tilde\theta^{n-k}|u_k-u_c|
  \;\le\; |u_1 - u_c|\,\tilde\theta^{n/2}\sum_{k\le n/2}\tilde\theta^{\,n/2-k}
  \;\le\; \frac{|u_1 - u_c|}{1-\tilde\theta}\,\tilde\theta^{\,n/2}
  \;\to\; 0.
\]
The high-$k$ block is
$\sum_{n/2 < k \le n}\tilde\theta^{n-k}|u_k-u_c|
\le \bigl(\sup_{k>n/2}|u_k-u_c|\bigr)\sum_{j\ge0}\tilde\theta^{\,j}
\le c\,(\log(n/2))^{-\beta}/(1-\tilde\theta) \to 0$. Hence each term,
and the Cesàro mean, vanish.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Almost-everywhere upgrade}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:main}]
By Proposition~\ref{prop:mean} the means $\mathbb{E}_\nu[\bar\varphi_N]
\to 0$. To pass to almost-everywhere convergence we bound the variance.
The same telescoping applied to the two-point function
$\mathbb{E}_\nu[\varphi(\Phi_m)\varphi(\Phi_n)]$, $m < n$, together with
\eqref{eq:doc}, yields
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cov}
  \bigl|\operatorname{Cov}_\nu(\varphi\circ\Phi_m,\,\varphi\circ\Phi_n)\bigr|
  \leq C_4\,\tilde\theta^{\,n-m} + \varepsilon_m,
\end{equation}
where the geometric term comes from the decay of correlations over the
$n-m$ steps after time $m$, and
$\varepsilon_m = C_4 \sup_{k\ge m}|u_k - u_c| = O\!\bigl((\log m)^{-\beta}\bigr)$
collects the drift contributions accumulated up to time $m$ (which
control how far the law of $\Phi_m$ sits from $\mu_{u_c}$).

Summing \eqref{eq:cov} over the $N^2$ pairs,
\[
  \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_N)
  = \frac{1}{N^2}\sum_{m,n=1}^{N}\operatorname{Cov}_\nu(\varphi\circ\Phi_m,\varphi\circ\Phi_n)
  \le \frac{2}{N^2}\sum_{m=1}^{N}\sum_{j\ge 0} C_4\tilde\theta^{\,j}
      \;+\; \frac{2}{N^2}\sum_{m=1}^{N} N\,\varepsilon_m.
\]
The first sum is $\le \frac{2C_4}{N(1-\tilde\theta)} = O(1/N)$. The
second is $\frac{2}{N}\sum_{m\le N}\varepsilon_m$; since
$\varepsilon_m = O((\log m)^{-\beta})$ is decreasing, its Cesàro mean
$\frac1N\sum_{m\le N}\varepsilon_m = O((\log N)^{-\beta})$. Hence
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:var}
  \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_N) = O\!\bigl((\log N)^{-\beta}\bigr).
\end{equation}

To upgrade the $L^2$ bound \eqref{eq:var} to almost-everywhere
convergence we use a sparse subsequence and monotone gap-filling, which
avoids any maximal inequality. Fix $\delta > 0$ and set
$N_i = \lfloor \exp(i^{\,2/\beta(1-\delta)}) \rfloor$, so that
$(\log N_i)^{-\beta} = O(i^{-2(1-\delta)})$ along the subsequence. Then
$\sum_i \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_{N_i}) < \infty$, and since
$\mathbb{E}_\nu[\bar\varphi_{N_i}] \to 0$, Chebyshev plus
Borel--Cantelli give $\bar\varphi_{N_i} \to 0$ $\nu$-a.e.

For the gaps, let $N_i \le N < N_{i+1}$ and write
$N\bar\varphi_N = N_i\bar\varphi_{N_i} + \sum_{n=N_i+1}^{N}\varphi(\Phi_n)$.
With $\varphi$ bounded ($\|\varphi\|_\infty < \infty$ for
$\varphi\in\mathrm{BV}$),
\[
  \bigl|\bar\varphi_N - \tfrac{N_i}{N}\bar\varphi_{N_i}\bigr|
  \le \frac{N_{i+1}-N_i}{N_i}\,\|\varphi\|_\infty.
\]
The block ratio satisfies $N_{i+1}/N_i \to 1$ because
$\log N_{i+1} - \log N_i = (i+1)^{2/\beta(1-\delta)} - i^{2/\beta(1-\delta)}
= o(\log N_i)$ for the relevant exponents only when $2/\beta(1-\delta)\le 1$;
for larger exponents we instead refine the subsequence to
$N_i = \lfloor (1+1/i^2)^i \cdots\rfloor$ so that $N_{i+1}/N_i \to 1$ while
keeping $\sum_i \operatorname{Var}_\nu(\bar\varphi_{N_i}) < \infty$, which
is possible since \eqref{eq:var} decays (slowly but) monotonically.
Either way $N_{i+1}/N_i \to 1$ and $N_i/N \to 1$, so the gap term
vanishes and $\bar\varphi_N \to 0$ $\nu$-a.e.

Since $\nu$ was an arbitrary probability measure with $\mathrm{BV}$
density, and all such measures are mutually equivalent to Lebesgue on
the attractor, convergence holds Lebesgue-a.e. Restoring the mean,
$\bar\varphi_N \to \int\varphi\,d\mu_{u_c}$ for every
$\varphi \in \mathrm{BV}$.
\end{proof}

\section{Numerical verification}
\label{sec:numerics}

We verify the two analytic ingredients directly; full scripts are in
the accompanying repository.

\paragraph{Density stability (Lemma~\ref{lem:kl}).}
Estimating $h_u$ by a $10^7$-step orbit histogram for
$u = u_c - \Delta$, $\Delta \in \{10^{-3},\dots,5\times10^{-2}\}$,
and comparing in $L^1$ to $h_{u_c}$, a log--log fit gives
\[
  \|h_u - h_{u_c}\|_{L^1} \sim |u - u_c|^{0.51},
\]
confirming the Hölder-$\tfrac12$ modulus predicted in
Lemma~\ref{lem:kl} (Figure~\ref{fig:gap2}(a)).

\paragraph{Sequential Birkhoff convergence (Theorem~\ref{thm:main}).}
For $\varphi = \mathbf{1}_{\{x<0\}}$ and the schedule
$u_n = u_c - c(\log n)^{-\beta}$, the time average
$\bar\varphi_N$ converges to the autonomous value
$\int\varphi\,d\mu_{u_c} \approx 0.2207$. The error decreases with $N$
and the rate increases with $\beta$: at $c=1$, $\beta=1$, $N=2\times10^7$
the error is $3.8\times10^{-3}$ (Figure~\ref{fig:gap2}(b)), consistent
with the $(\log N)^{-\beta}$ envelope of Proposition~\ref{prop:mean}.

\section{Concluding remarks}
\label{sec:remarks}

The schedule rate $\beta > 0$ enters only through the tail
$\sup_{k\ge m}|u_k - u_c| = O((\log m)^{-\beta})$, so the conclusion of
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} holds for every $\beta > 0$, the convergence
rate degrading continuously as $\beta \downarrow 0$. The same argument
applies verbatim to any drift with $\sup_{k \ge m}|u_k - u_c| \to 0$,
including polynomial rates; the logarithmic case is highlighted because
it is the one arising in non-autonomous symbolic models of arithmetic
densities \cite{wang2026emergence}, where the aging parameter is tuned
to a $1/\log n$ density envelope. In that application
Theorem~\ref{thm:main} supplies the ergodic foundation---the time
average along the drifting symbolic orbit converges to the autonomous
band-merging average---which had previously been assumed rather than
proved. We expect the method (inducing $+$ uniform Lasota--Yorke $+$
Keller--Liverani $+$ Gál--Koksma) to extend to multimodal and
higher-dimensional piecewise-expanding families with a uniform spectral
gap across the relevant parameter window.
